# Cumulus Bank — Portfolio Product Specifications
Effective April 25, 2026. All rates, limits, and terms below are illustrative-only for a fictitious bank but internally consistent across the portfolio. Agents authoring brochures MUST use these values verbatim so rates line up across related products (e.g., CD ladder, HYSA vs MMA, credit-card APR ranges).

## Global assumptions
- Prime Rate (illustrative): **8.00% APR** as of the effective date.
- 10-Year Treasury (illustrative): **4.10%** — used in mortgage APR assumptions.
- FDIC limit: $250,000 per depositor, per insured institution, per ownership category.
- SIPC limit: $500,000 of which $250,000 may be cash, for investment products through Cumulus Investment Services, LLC.
- All consumer deposit accounts use daily-balance interest accrual, monthly credit.
- All lending subject to credit approval, UW, verification (income, employment, assets, collateral).
- NMLS #2026045 on all consumer lending.

## Voice guide per segment
- **Retail** — confident, warm, professional. "Open an account", "Apply today", "Speak with a banker". Active voice.
- **Wealth** — editorial, measured, advisory. "Discuss your objectives", "Your advisor will", "Consult your tax professional". No salesy language.
- **Commercial** — institutional, B2B. "Request a proposal", "Engage a Treasury specialist", "Relationship Manager". Precise language around covenants, collateral, documentation.

## Brochure structure (all products)

Each brochure should include these sections in order (adapt length per product complexity):

1. **Cover** (auto — use `hero_block()` with `category_label="PRODUCT BROCHURE  ·  <CATEGORY>"`)
2. **Overview** (`section_header(kicker="At a glance")`) — 1 short lead paragraph
3. **Key benefits** (`feature_grid`, 4–6 cells) — 4–6 benefits with short descriptions
4. **Rates / Pricing / Fees** — use `data_table` for rate cards and fee schedules
5. **At least one chart** — `growth_curve_chart` for deposits, `amortization_chart` for loans, `bar_comparison_chart` for rate tier comparisons, `donut_chart` for portfolio/allocation products
6. **Eligibility & application / Underwriting** — two_col of "Who qualifies" + "Documentation required"
7. **How it works** — numbered step table
8. **Limits / capabilities / features table** — where applicable
9. **Security / regulatory protections** — relevant regs (DD/E/CC/Z/X/B/P/GG)
10. **FAQ** — 4–6 Q&A (use `KeepTogether` per Q&A)
11. **Disclosures** — `disclosure_block` with appropriate STANDARD_*_DISCLOSURES list + product-specific
12. **Back cover** — `back_cover_block()` (auto per segment)

Target 4–7 pages per product. Wealth and Commercial products may run 6–8 pages. Simple products (e.g., Secured Card) can be 3–4 pages.

## Offer document structure (all products)

Offer documents are campaign companions to the brochures. They are generated by
`generator/generate_offers.py`, use the same `generator/brand.py` document
system, and must preserve the base product economics listed in this spec.
Campaign incentives may add temporary pricing, rate, bonus, fee-waiver, or
implementation concessions, but they must not contradict the product terms.

Each offer should include these sections in order:

1. **Cover** (`hero_block()` with `category_label="CAMPAIGN OFFER  -  <CATEGORY>"`) - campaign name, window, base terms, incentive, offer code, and fulfillment rule
2. **Campaign overview** - concise positioning and primary client promise
3. **Current offer economics** - standard product terms vs. campaign concession vs. client value
4. **Qualification and fulfillment** - campaign window, qualifying action, minimum requirement, verification, and fulfillment timing
5. **FSC campaign playbook** - best-fit audiences, trigger events, and next best actions
6. **Client journey** - identify, present, validate, open/book/implement, fulfill, deepen
7. **Controls and disclosure guardrails** - rate basis, offer authority, FSC evidence, adverse-change language, demo-use control
8. **Offer disclosures** - product-family standard disclosures plus product-specific campaign disclosures
9. **Back cover** - `back_cover_block()` (auto per segment)

Target 7-9 pages per offer. Offer PDFs should remain campaign documents, not
replacement product brochures.
